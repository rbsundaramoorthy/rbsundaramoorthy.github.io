---
name: "Threadbare"
description: "A minimal journaling app that asks one unexpected question a day."
status: "Active"
tech: "Astro, SQLite"
---

Threadbare is built on a simple premise: most journaling apps fail because they give you a blank page. A blank page is an invitation to write nothing. Instead, Threadbare asks you one carefully crafted question each day — questions designed to surface thoughts you didn't know you had.

The question bank draws from philosophy, psychology, and everyday observation. "What did you almost say today but didn't?" "What's something you're pretending to understand?" "If you could only keep three possessions, what would they be and why?"

The tech stack is deliberately simple. Astro for the frontend, SQLite for local-first data storage. No cloud sync, no social features, no analytics. Just you and a question.
